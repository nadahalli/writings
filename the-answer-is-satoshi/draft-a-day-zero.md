# Draft A: Day Zero

## Structure

First person. January 3, 2009. The story starts in the morning and ends with the genesis block. One day, dense, real-time.

## The Day

The narrator wakes up. Normal morning. Coffee. The machines in the other room have been there for weeks, tested, ready. He's been building toward this for two years. Today is the day, but the story doesn't say that. He just... starts his morning.

He goes through routines. Maybe he has a day job (we never learn what). Maybe it's a Saturday and he doesn't. The machines hum. He checks them the way you'd check a generator before a storm.

At some point, he picks up a newspaper. Or loads one online. He's looking for something, but the story doesn't say what. He finds a headline: "Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks." He pauses on it. Maybe re-reads it. The normal reader thinks he's interested in the news. The Bitcoiner's hair stands up.

Through the day, small details accumulate:
- He re-reads code he wrote. Winces at parts of it. It's not elegant. It works.
- He drafts an email. Re-reads it. Deletes a sentence that's too personal. Rewrites it colder.
- He checks hardware. Not with excitement. With the care of someone who has already checked many times.
- He alternates British and American spelling in something he's writing. Deliberately.
- A book is visible somewhere. Hayek, maybe. Or it's been there so long it's furniture.
- He thinks about a problem involving trust. Not abstractly. Specifically. A sentence keeps forming in his mind, something about "trusted third parties."
- He calculates something. Hashrate, difficulty, time. The math is simple. Middle school arithmetic. But what it implies is not.

Late in the day (or evening), he does the thing. The machines start working on something. A block. The first block. The headline is in it. The story doesn't explain what a block is or what the headline means in this context. It just shows a man watching a screen, waiting, and then a number appearing.

## Ending Options

- The number 0 appears on screen. He exhales. End.
- The last line is the embedded text: "The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks." No explanation.
- He closes the laptop, or turns off the monitor, and goes to bed. The machines keep humming.
- He opens an email client and begins typing: "I've been working on a new electronic cash system..."

## Tone

Quiet. Methodical. A person alone with something enormous, treating it like Tuesday. The tension is entirely subterranean.

## Risks

- The newspaper/headline moment might be too on-the-nose for Bitcoiners.
- One day might not be enough to show the preparation (hardware, code, intellectual lineage).
- The ending needs to work for someone who doesn't know what a genesis block is.

## Easter Eggs

- The newspaper headline (obvious to Bitcoiners)
- The hash calculation / difficulty math
- The machines (mining hardware before mining existed as a concept)
- The email draft discipline (no personal details, no timezone leaks)
- British/American spelling alternation
- The phrase "trusted third party" appearing as an irritant
- Hayek or similar on a shelf
- A printed paper on the desk, maybe with "hashcash" or "b-money" in the title
- He's alone. Completely alone. No one knows what he's doing.
